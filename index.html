Net Neutrality

Posted on 28 Jun 2014 by Kyle Daugherty

So, just what is Net Neutrality? Net Neutrality is all about keeping the Internet open for everyone. It means that Internet service providers (ISPs), like Comcast and Time Warner Cable, must treat all data passed over its network equally, regardless of the content or who sent it or who is receiving it. ISPs should not be allowed to give preference to one site over another. This principle enables and protects our right to communicate freely online.
